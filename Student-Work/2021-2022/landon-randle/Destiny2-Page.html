Destiny 2 is a fun PvE and PvP experience. There are many different ways to play the game, I will start with the best way to step up your game in PvE, then move onto PvP
PvE is a major part of the storyline of Destiny 2. Almost every aspect of the story mode is PvE (Except for the Crucible, which I will get to later.) Right now, there are a total of 5 planets to explore throughout the story, all of which have different mechanics and enemies. Here are the best steps for the PvE experience:
 1 - Go through the tutorial all the way up until you meet the main characters in the tower
2 - Go right into the Shadowkeep missions on the moon.
3 - Complete each section of the Shadowkeep until you have a full set of Dreambane armor
4 - Grind legendary shards until you reach at least 60 or 70
5 - Wait for Xur to appear on the weekends
6 - Grab a good exotic weapon or armor piece from him (each one is 23 legendary shards)
7 - Finally, practice until you are ready for Nightfall Strikes

Next we have PvP. PvP is one of the less time consuming parts of Destiny 2. Most of these steps will be on how to step up your game and get better. (Tip: don't start competitive PvP until you have the full set of Dreambane armor and/or an exotic armor piece)
 1 - Go into the Crucible and pick 3v3 Elimination. It is the best way to practice without doing any damage to your K/D.
2 - Always have a weapon matching your subclass, and another than is the opposite range strength of your other weapon. (hand cannon with smg, assault rifle with sniper, pulse rifle with shotgun, etc.)
3 - If you're a Titan, place shields at every corner where you can peak. If you're a Warlock, place a healing rift behind every Titan shield. If you're a Hunter, dodge and turn invisible every kill, and always go around the enemy while they're distracted
4 - If you're a Titan, avoid wide open spaces. If you're a Warlock, avoid peaking corners. If you're a Hunter, avoid all enemy rifts and shields.
